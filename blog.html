Nguyên

I gave my whole attention to satisfying a want that consumed me for a number of years. I sacrificed every aspect of my life for my career, including holidays, weekends, and sleep, in order to succeed. A sense of rebirth was initially brought forth by the drive and ambition, but as time passed, that initial excitement began to wane. I was tired and unable to maintain the standard I had set for myself, a result of the toll that the relentless pressure and lengthy hours had taken. Although I was quite exhausted, I still wouldn't slow down. I would not let myself become bogged down by laziness; I was hell-bent on succeeding in my endeavors. I persisted in giving it my all day after day, certain that my perseverance would pay off in the end. Unfortunately, I never got the recognition or compensation I had hoped for, no matter how hard I tried. As the months turned into years after I started working relentlessly, it seemed like my efforts were producing diminishing returns. Where there was once a raging inferno of want to achieve my goals, there is now a growing sense of despair. I began to doubt the value of my sacrifices, lose faith in the outcome, and wonder if my efforts had been in vain. Once upon a time, the dream seemed so within reach; today, it seemed like an impossible pipe dream. [08:49] After another long day at work, I returned to my flat to sit alone and think on the adventure I had just finished. "Everything in life depends on how that life accepts its limits." That was a profound realization that struck me during that serene meditation practice. Because I had been so focused on testing my boundaries, I failed to see how important it was to really understand and appreciate them. I began to see the situation for what it truly was after acquiring this new perspective. My decision to give up was not indicative of failure so much as of acknowledging my limitations and prioritizing my own health. I learned self-care, set limits, and could finally say "no" to people when they asked me to do too much. Gradually releasing the impossible standards I had set for myself led me to a place of peace I'd been seeking for a long time. The simple joys of life were rediscovery, my hobbies and passions were reignited, and I reconnected with the people that matter most to me. My profession has developed into only one facet of a rich and fulfilling existence throughout the years. Rather than embracing failure, the process of learning to give up entailed finding strength in accepting the reality. Real happiness and contentment were within my reach once I accepted and acknowledged my limitations.
