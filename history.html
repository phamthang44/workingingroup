History of Forming/Establishing

Our journey begin at the moment that we arrived at the information technology department for the very first time, we immediately gathered together and formed a collaborative group. To begin, our first purpose was to complete the POSE assignment, which stands for Principles of Software Engineering. This was our initial objective. In addition, it was the very first time that we had ever before had challenges associated with passing the subject matter that was being considered. Due to a lack of talent, we have been presented with a myriad of problems, particularly in the fields of team working, knowledge, labour, and leisure. These problems have arisen as a direct consequence of the deficit of talent. We did not pay any attention to the lecture, and we did not begin working on the assignment until it was very near to the time that it was due. In addition, we did not pay any attention even to the lecture. The conclusion that we reached was that we had made a mistake. Due to the fact that the results of the exam left us feeling so disheartened, we made the decision to pursue additional schooling after we had finished the test.

Since we hadn’t known how to work as a group, so we didn’t even give our group a name. The time come when we attent to “Working in group” lectures, at the first thing ever the teacher told us to do is making a team. We easily gatther around and again, froming a team. The interesting part is when the teacher told us introduce ourself to the class, which included our name, sologan. You might guess it, we struggled to choose and pick up a name for our group too. Thành, a member of our group have one-sided pick “Sciocoo” as our group’s team. We believed he was making fun of us until he explained why he felt "Sciocoo" was the best name for us; until then, we didn't understand what he meant when he stated it meant dumb in Italy.

Why we choose this name "Scicco" ?

"Stupid" is something that the term itself means. The reason why he chose this name, on the other hand, seemed to us as being quite plausible. In the beginning, the reason why our group was given this name was because the more you studied, the more you immersed yourself in the ocean of knowledge, the more you felt like you were drowning. However, despite the fact that you are acquiring an ever-increasing amount of knowledge and improving your skills, you will have the impression that you were previously ignorant and that your current level of knowledge is only a drop of water in comparison to the vast amount of information that you are capable of acquiring eventually. Moreover, he even show us the “Dunning–Kruger effect” and states that it’s the best to see yourself as a fools, a dummy in order to achieve more and more knowledge. In point of fact, the reason why we decided to go with his suggestion is that, as we said before, we did not truly commit much effort to studying over the previous terms. The processes that we have engaged in throughout the class include engaging with pursuits such as "mở server đi ông," playing games, and sleeping. Following the receipt of the outcome as a consequence of that, we come to the realisation that we were really dumb. How did we squander all of our time and knowledge and end up with a regrettable situation?

Our only subsequent action was to decide to take ourselves seriously and begin studying in the right way, which is something we should have been doing for months. We must make an even greater commitment to research and analysis in order to comprehend how we compare to other people if we are to recover from that setback. In the time after, Thành re-believed that we are the fool ones who have an attraction to knowledge or can be called thirsty for knowledge and always want to do more and more assignments so that "the fool" might reach their prior level of superiority. Thành has had this belief towards us.
